Flash Instructions

1
Download the .img.xz file above. No need to extract — Raspberry Pi Imager and dd both handle compressed images.
2
Option A — Raspberry Pi Imager (easiest): Open Imager, choose "Use custom image", select the .img.xz, pick your SD card, and write.
3
Option B — dd (Linux/macOS): 
$ xz -d mintkit-pizero2w.img.xz
$ sudo dd if=mintkit-pizero2w.img of=/dev/sdX bs=4M status=progress
$ sync
Replace /dev/sdX with your SD card device (check with lsblk).
4
Insert SD card into Pi Zero 2 W and power on. MintKit boots straight to the launcher in ~15 seconds.
